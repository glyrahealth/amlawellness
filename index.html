FAQ

Things people ask.

Who is eligible for compounded GLP-1 treatment?

Eligibility is determined by a licensed clinician after reviewing your intake questionnaire. Generally, adult patients with a BMI ≥ 27 (or ≥ 30 without comorbidities) and no contraindications can be considered. The final decision is made by your provider, not by AmlaWellness.

What's in compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide?

The active ingredient is the same molecule found in the brand-name product, sourced from FDA-registered facilities. Compounded products are prepared by a state-licensed compounding pharmacy and are not FDA-approved. The FDA does not evaluate compounded products for safety or efficacy.

How much does it cost — and do you take insurance?

We operate on transparent cash-pay pricing, no insurance required. Plans start at $199/mo for compounded semaglutide and $349/mo for tirzepatide. Pricing includes the clinician consultation, ongoing care, and medication.

What states do you ship to?

We currently support patients in 48 U.S. states. State-specific licensing may affect treatment availability — your eligibility will be confirmed during intake.

How is the medication shipped?

Your prescription ships in temperature-controlled cold-chain packaging directly from the partner pharmacy. Tracking is provided. Most patients receive their first shipment within 5–7 business days of approval.

What ongoing support is included?

Your plan includes secure messaging with your clinician, dose-titration guidance, side-effect support, and monthly check-ins. You'll have access to our patient portal 24/7.

Can I cancel any time?

Yes. There are no long-term commitments. You can pause or cancel your subscription from your patient portal between refills.

Is this the same as Ozempic® or Wegovy®?

No. Ozempic®, Wegovy®, Mounjaro®, and Zepbound® are FDA-approved brand-name products manufactured by their respective companies and are not affiliated with AmlaWellness. Our compounded products contain the same active ingredient but are prepared by a compounding pharmacy and are not FDA-approved.
